About The Bad Homosexual

In the fall of 2019, I had the privilege of taking a queer literature class at the University of Michigan with David M. Halperin, founder and editor of GLQ magazine and Gay Shame as well as author of many quintessential queer books such as Saint Foucault. That class was labeled in my course guide as Bad Homosexuals, and under David's tutelage my classmates and I read and dissected the intricacies of what it means to be represented in literature, and what kinds of depictions do harm versus good for LGBTQ+ progress. As a newly-out lesbian, an anarcho-communist and a radical transgender rights advocate, I was struggling to find my footing in a community where I felt my radical leftism, my sexual punk aesthetic and attitude, and my struggles with mental illness were looked down upon. David M. Halperin's class was a place where I felt at home with the likes of Jean Genet, Dennis Cooper and Sarah Schulman, taking comfort in a literary canon of queerness where queer people were allowed to be radical, poor, trans, emotional, sexual, imperfect--and otherwise "bad".

Inspired by Diana DiMassa's Hothead Paisan, I started this project on an Instagram page of the same name, using black and white comics and one-paneled pages to vent my frustrations about being queer (and being "bad") in today's climate, one where heternormativity, cisnormativity, capitalism and racism reign supreme. It has grown into a long-term project that I hope will reach many other "bad" queers who want to find solace in doing queerness incorrectly.
